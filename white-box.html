White Box Study

Some details about the White Box study data set

The White Box study paper (and this additional paper about it) discusses and analyzes the White Box data set. The data is stored in an Excel sheet, and there are \(8\) tabs in it. Those different tabs are the data sets shown below.

WhiteBox_Survey

Variables
  1. 4.0: gender of examiner
  • ex: Female, Male
  1. 2. Age: age range of examiner
  • ex: \(30-39\), \(40-49\)
  1. 3. Education: education level of examiner
  • ex: Grad, Assoc, Bachelor, HS
  1. 4. Employer: employer of examiner
  • ex: U.S. City/County government, Non-U.S. National/Federal government, U.S. State government, U.S. Federal government
  1. 5. Agency accred.: whether examiner is accredited by agency
  • ex: Yes, No
  1. 6. Years employed: range of years examiner was employed
  • ex: \(5-9\), \(10-19\)
  1. 7. Training: examiners’ form of training
  • ex: Limited formal, Formal halfYear, Formal yr+, Other
  1. 8. IAI-cert:
  • ex: IAI, NA
  1. 8. Employer-cert:
  • ex: Employer, NA
  1. 8. National-cert:
  • ex: National, NA
  1. 8. Other-cert:
  • ex: Other, NA
  1. 9. Active:
  • ex: Yes, previously regular, No
  1. 10. Time spent:
  • ex: \(\text{Less than }10\%\), \(10-25\%\), \(25-50\%\), \(50-75\%\), \(75-100\%\)
  1. 11. Computer-based:
  • ex: \(0\% \text{ computer}\), \(1-30\% \text{ computer}\), \(30-60\% \text{ computer}\), \(60-99\% \text{ computer}\), \(100\% \text{ computer}\)
  1. 12. Single exemplar:
  • ex: \(\text{None}\), \(\text{Less than } 10\%\), \(10-25\%\), \(25-50\%\), \(50-75\%\), \(75-100\%\)
  1. 13. Limited match:
  • ex: Yes, No
  1. 14. VEOs:
  • ex: \(\text{its own category (standard)}\), \(\text{its own category (request)}\), \(\text{Of value}\), \(\text{No value}\)
  1. 15. AFIS value:
  • ex: \(\text{Yes, used as formal designation}\), \(\text{Yes, used informally}\), \(\text{No}\)
  1. 16. Test latents similar?:
  • ex: \(\text{Some of them}\), \(\text{Most of them}\), \(\text{All of them}\)
  1. 17. Exemplars similar?:
  • ex: \(\text{Some of them}\), \(\text{Most of them}\), \(\text{All of them}\), \(\text{All but one or two}\)
  1. 18. Compare to casework:
  • ex: \(\text{MUCH EASIER than casework}\), \(\text{EASIER than casework}\), \(\text{SIMILAR to casework}\), \(\text{HARDER than casework}\), \(\text{MUCH HARDER than casework}\)
  1. 19. Markup useful?:
  • ex: \(\text{Possibly}\), \(\text{Yes, for general practice}\), \(\text{Yes, Only for complex cases}\), \(\text{No}\)
  1. 20. PointStandardCount:
  • ex: \(16\), NA

WhiteBox_Determinations

Variables
  1. Pair_ID: unique identifier for the image pair
  • ex: \(M010003\)
  1. Examiner (Anon): unique, anonymized identifier for the examiner
  • ex: \(EA1338\)
  1. Mating: whether the latent and exemplar originate from the same source
  • ex: Mates, Non-mates
  1. Latent Value (Analysis):
  • ex: VID, VEO, NV
  1. Latent Value (Comparison):
  • ex: VID, VEO, NV
  1. Exemplar Value:
  • ex: VALUE
  1. Comparison Determination: how examiner identified comparison (or not)
  • ex: ID, Exclusion, Inconc, None
  1. Difficulty: examiner’s rating of difficulty to make comparison decision
  • ex: VERYEASY, EASY, MODERATE, DIFFICULT, VERYDIFFICULT
  1. Latent minutiae: number of minutiae found by examiner on latent print
  • ex: \(31\)
  1. Latent cores: number of cores found by examiner on latent print
  • ex: \(1\)
  1. Latent deltas: number of delta areas found by examiner on latent print
  • ex: \(0\), \(1\)
  1. Latent points:
  • ex: \(0\), \(2\)
  1. Exemplar minutiae: number of minutiae found by examiner on exemplar print
  • ex: \(19\)
  1. Corresp minutiae: number of minutiae found altogether by examiner on corresponding latent and exemplar prints
  • ex: \(19\)
  1. Corresp core: number of cores found altogether by examiner on corresponding latent and exemplar prints
  • ex: \(1\)
  1. Corresp delta: number of delta areas found altogether by examiner on corresponding latent and exemplar prints
  • ex: \(0\), \(1\)
  1. Corresp points:
  • ex: \(0\), \(2\)

WhiteBox_Markup

Variables
  1. Pair_ID: unique identifier for the image pair
  • ex: \(M010003\)
  1. Examiner (Anon): unique, anonymized identifier for the examiner
  • ex: \(EA1338\)
  1. Print: whether print is latent or exemplar
  • ex: Latent, Exemplar
  1. Stage: whether stage is analysis or comparison
  • ex: Analysis, Comparison
  1. CorrespondenceType:
  • ex: NA, DebatableFeature
  1. LType: latent print type
  • ex: minutia, core, NA
  1. Latent_X: X coordinate of latent minutiae
  • ex: \(68\)
  1. Latent_Y: Y coordinate of latent minutiae
  • ex: \(705\)
  1. Latent_Quality: quality of latent print
  • ex: \(3\)
  1. EType: exemplar print type
  • ex: NA, minutia, core, delta
  1. Exemplar_X: X coordinate of exemplar minutiae
  • ex: NA, \(172\)
  1. Exemplar_Y: Y coordinate of exemplar minutiae
  • ex: NA, \(222\)
  1. Exemplar_Quality: quality of exemplar print
  • ex: NA, \(3\)
  1. Corresponding Clarity: minimum of Latent_Quality and Exemplar_Quality
  • ex: NA, \(3\)
